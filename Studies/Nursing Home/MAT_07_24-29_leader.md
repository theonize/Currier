# Leader's Notes — Matthew 7:24-29 - Wise and Foolish Builders

## Verses

> **24** "Everyone then who hears these words of mine and does them will be like a wise man who built his house on the rock.
>
> **25** And the rain fell, and the floods came, and the winds blew and beat on that house, but it did not fall, because it had been founded on the rock.
>
> **26** And everyone who hears these words of mine and does not do them will be like a foolish man who built his house on the sand.
>
> **27** And the rain fell, and the floods came, and the winds blew and beat against that house, and it fell, and great was the fall of it."
>
> **28** And when Jesus finished these sayings, the crowds were astonished at his teaching,
>
> **29** for he was teaching them as one who had authority, and not as their scribes.
>
> — *English Standard Version (ESV)*

---

## Overview

- **Setting** — Galilee, c. AD 28-30, on the hillside above the northwest shore of the Sea of Galilee (traditional "Mount of Beatitudes" near Capernaum).  Under Herod Antipas, Second Temple period; an agrarian, near-subsistence society where a collapsed house meant ruin.
- **Speaker / author** — Jesus, the authoritative teacher of the kingdom, presented by Matthew (the tax collector, Matthew 9:9) as a "new Moses" delivering covenant instruction from a mountain.
- **Immediate context** — These verses are the deliberate **conclusion of the Sermon on the Mount** (Matthew 5-7).  The narrator's frame (vv.28-29) forms an *inclusio* with 5:1-2 (Jesus went up, sat, and taught).
- **Genre** — A **parabolic similitude** ("will be like...") inside a wisdom discourse, capped by a short narrative report.  Functions rhetorically as the **peroratio** — the closing appeal — of the whole Sermon.
- **Literary frame** — The climax of a chain of paired alternatives that structures 7:13-27:
  - two gates / two ways (13-14)
  - two trees / two fruits (15-20)
  - two confessions — "Lord, Lord" / "I never knew you" (21-23)
  - **two builders / two houses (24-27)**
- **Big idea** — *A life endures the storm of testing and judgment only if built on the rock of hearing **and doing** Jesus' words; hearing without doing is fatal self-deception.*
- **Supporting movements:**
  - Both builders hear the *same* words and face the *identical* storm; only the **foundation** (rock vs. sand) and the **outcome** (stood vs. fell) differ.
  - The single decisive variable is **doing** versus **not doing** what Jesus says.
  - The pluperfect "had been founded" (v.25) shows the storm *reveals* — it does not create — what earlier obedience established.
  - The crowd's astonishment (vv.28-29) exposes the real scandal: Jesus teaches with inherent **authority** (ἐξουσία), making obedience to *his own words* the criterion of survival — an implicit claim to divine status.

## Info

### v.24
- **"Everyone then"** — πᾶς + inferential οὖν ties this directly to 7:21-23 ("Lord, Lord" / "I never knew you"); leaves no neutral third option.
- **"these words of mine"** (τοὺς λόγους μου) — the entire Sermon (chs. 5-7); *inclusio* with 5:2.  Jesus places **his own words** where Torah and wisdom stood — implicit Christology.
- **"hears... and does"** — **ἀκούει** (G191) + **ποιεῖ** (G4160); the hinge of the parable.  Semitic "hear" = obey (the Shema, Deuteronomy 6:4).
- **"wise"** — **φρόνιμος** (G5429): practical prudence, skill in living, not mere intellect; same word as the wise virgins (Matthew 25:2).
- **"will be like"** — **ὁμοιωθήσεται**, future passive ("will be likened"); the tense points forward to how one *will be assessed* on the day of judgment.
- **"rock"** — **πέτρα** (G4073): bedrock; anticipates 16:18 ("on this rock I will build my church," same verb **οἰκοδομέω**).

### v.25
- **Three assaults** — "rain... floods... winds": a threefold completeness of testing; nothing is held back.  **βροχή** ("rain," G1028) appears in the NT *only* here and v.27.
- **Parataxis** — clauses hammered together with καί; the grammar enacts the relentlessness of the storm.
- **"it did not fall"** — outcome one; deliverance through the storm, not from it.
- **"had been founded"** — **τεθεμελίωτο**, pluperfect passive: the foundation was a *settled, prior fact*.  The house stood **because** the foundation-laying belonged to the past — present obedience establishes future endurance.

### v.26
- An almost exact **mirror of v.24**, diverging only at "does **not** do them" and "**foolish**."
- **"foolish"** — **μωρός** (G3474): dull, senseless (source of "moron"); echoes salt that loses its savor (5:13, μωρανθῇ) and the forbidden insult "you fool" (5:22, μωρέ).
- Both builders **hear the identical words**; the only difference is the doing.
- **"sand"** — **ἄμμος** (G285): loose wadi alluvium — level, accessible, easy to build on, but shifting.

### v.27
- The **same storm**, narrated in near-verbatim parallel to v.25 (note ESV's subtle shift: "beat **on**" in v.25, "beat **against**" in v.27).
- **"great was the fall of it"** — **πτῶσις μεγάλη** (G4431); the heaviest word is held to the very end (end-stress); the sentence falls as the house falls.
- **Irony** — the foolish builder is equally diligent and his house equally finished; effort without obedience is wasted.

### v.28
- **"when Jesus finished these sayings"** — the **first of Matthew's five discourse-closing formulas** (7:28; 11:1; 13:53; 19:1; 26:1) that divide the Gospel into five teaching blocks — Jesus as the new Moses, his teaching a new Torah.
- Forms an *inclusio* with 5:1-2, sealing the Sermon.
- **"astonished"** — **ἐξεπλήσσοντο** (G1605): "struck out," overwhelmed; an ongoing, powerful reaction.

### v.29
- **"as one who had authority"** — **ἐξουσία** (G1849): inherent, self-authenticating; Jesus says "I say to you," not "Rabbi X said."
- **"not as their scribes"** — scribal authority was *derivative*, citing the chain of tradition.  The possessive **"their"** (αὐτῶν) distances Matthew's community from the scribal establishment.
- **Textual note** — some manuscripts add "and the Pharisees"; this is a harmonizing expansion and is rejected by the critical text.

## Discussion

### Observation
1. What do the two builders have in common, and what is the *single* thing that finally separates them?
   - **Leader's note:**  Both hear the same words, both build, both face the identical storm (vv.24-27).  Only the foundation (rock/sand) and the outcome (stood/fell) differ.  Drive the group to isolate the lone variable: *doing* versus *not doing*.
2. List the forces in the storm (vv.25, 27). Does either house get a gentler storm?
   - **Leader's note:** Rain, floods, winds — identical in both panels.  The same test comes to everyone; the foundation, not the absence of the storm, decides the outcome.
3. How does Matthew describe the crowd's response, and what reason does he give (vv.28-29)?
   - **Leader's note:** "Astonished" (ἐξεπλήσσοντο) because Jesus taught with authority (ἐξουσία), "not as their scribes" — direct vs. derivative authority.

### Interpretation
4. What exactly are "these words of mine" (vv.24, 26)?
   - **Leader's note:** The whole Sermon on the Mount (chs. 5-7); *inclusio* with 5:2.  Note the staggering move: Jesus puts his own words in the load-bearing place wisdom reserved for Torah and the fear of the LORD — implicit Christology.
5. Why "wise / foolish" rather than "believer / unbeliever"?
   - **Leader's note:** φρόνιμος / μωρός is wisdom vocabulary (cf. Proverbs; the ten virgins, Matthew 25:1-13).  Folly here is not lack of effort or intelligence but the refusal to anchor one's life in God's word.
6. What is the "rock," and what is the storm?
   - **Leader's note:** The rock is Jesus' words obeyed — ultimately Christ himself (16:18; 1 Corinthians 3:11).  The storm is testing and final judgment (cf. 7:21-23; Ezekiel 13:10-16).
7. Why does the grammar "had been founded" (pluperfect, v.25) matter?
   - **Leader's note:** The foundation was set *before* the storm.  Testing only reveals what earlier obedience established; present foundations settle future outcomes.

### Application
8. Where do I currently *know* a command of Jesus but am not *doing* it?
   - The hearing-doing gap is the passage's central target.
9. Is my Christianity mostly *auditory* — sermons, books, podcasts — with little obedience that costs me anything?
   - Consumption is not discipleship; "their scribes" models secondhand, derivative religion.
10. What in my life looks finished and respectable but has never been tested at its foundation?
    - Connect to the honor-shame point: both houses look fine; the storm exposes the hidden foundation.  Self-examination before the storm, not during it.
11. What is one specific change this week that converts hearing into doing?
    - Meaning vs. method — the principle is fixed (faith that *does* what Jesus says); the method flexes by person and season.
