# Leader's Notes — Psalms 145:14-21 - The Lord upholds all who fall

## Verses

> **14** The LORD upholds all who are falling and raises up all who are bowed down.
>
> **15** The eyes of all look to you, and you give them their food in due season.
>
> **16** You open your hand; you satisfy the desire of every living thing.
>
> **17** The LORD is righteous in all his ways and kind in all his works.
>
> **18** The LORD is near to all who call on him, to all who call on him in truth.
>
> **19** He fulfills the desire of those who fear him; he also hears their cry and saves them.
>
> **20** The LORD preserves all who love him, but all the wicked he will destroy.
>
> **21** My mouth will speak the praise of the LORD, and let all flesh bless his holy name forever and ever.
>
> -- *English Standard Version (ESV)*

---

## Overview

- **Setting** — The closing eight-letter run (samek-tav) of David's acrostic. Implicit venue: the temple where "my mouth will speak" (v.21); scope: the whole world.
- **Author / speaker** — David, the shepherd-king who once guarded sheep from lion and bear (1 Sam 17), describing his King doing the same for "all flesh"; lifted up after his own falls (Pss 32, 51).
- **Position in the book** — End of David's last superscribed psalm; *seeds* every theme that blooms in the closing Hallel (Pss 146-150). The final pentad is commentary on Psalm 145.
- **Genre / frame** — Closing movement of a descriptive-praise hymn; providence hymn + compressed salvation language + wisdom's two-ways + doxological coda.
- **Big idea** — YHWH is the universal, ever-active Provider whose righteous-and-kind character upholds the helpless, hears honest prayer, and draws all flesh into praise.
- **Supporting movements:**
  - Register shifts from royal (vv.1-13) to *pastoral* — upholds, feeds, hears, saves, preserves.
  - **Participial saturation** (*somek, zoqef, potech, shomer*) = YHWH's continuous, habitual action.
  - Anatomical spine — eyes (v.15) → hand (v.16) → mouth (v.21).
  - **כֹּל** (*kol*, "all") appears ~11× in 8 verses, hammering universal scope.
  - v.17 ("righteous and kind") mirrors the vv.8-9 credo — twin character-pillars under the providence claim.

---

## Info

### v.14
- **סוֹמֵךְ** (*somek*, "upholds") + **זוֹקֵף** (*zoqef*, "raises") — active participles = continuous action; *samak* = to take weight, lean upon (Lev 1:4; Num 27:18).
- **כְּפוּפִים** (*kefufim*, "bowed down") — the burdened: widows, orphans, debt-slaves, elderly, disabled in a society with no welfare net. Not poetry — survival.

### v.15
- **שָׂבֵר** (*saber*) — "eyes of all look/wait hopefully"; idiom *nasa' enayim*, to lift the eyes = to hope (Gen 13:10; Ps 121:1).
- "Food in due season" (*be'itto*) — agrarian existence; YHWH sovereign over the harvest calendar that pagans credited to Baal/Hadad/Dagon.
- Heart of the Jewish grace-after-meals (*Birkat Hamazon*).

### v.16
- **פּוֹתֵחַ אֶת־יָדֶךָ** (*potech et-yadekha*), "you open your hand" — ANE "deity's hand" provision motif (cf. Aten's ray-hands), here democratized to "every living thing."
- **רָצוֹן** (*ratson*, "desire/favor") — creaturely desire met because YHWH's will is gracious.
- Canonical node on the "open hand" artery: Eden → manna → feeding miracles → Rev 7:16-17.

### v.17
- **צַדִּיק** (*tsaddiq*, "righteous") + **חָסִיד** (*chasid*, "kind," from the *chesed* root) — YHWH is himself *chasid*, not merely the patron of *chasidim*.
- Parallel character-credo to vv.8-9; providence grounded in revealed character, not sentiment.

### v.18
- Paronomasia **קָרוֹב / קֹרְאָיו** (*qarov / qor'ayw*), "near / callers" — the sound enacts the nearness it announces.
- **בֶּאֱמֶת** (*be'emet*), "in truth" — sincere covenant prayer vs. lip-honoring (Isa 29:13); the only qualifier — not pedigree or office.

### v.19
- Three imperfects cascade — fulfills / hears / saves. **שַׁוְעָה** (*shav'ah*) = cry of distress.
- "Those who fear him" — *yir'at YHWH*, wisdom's central virtue (Prov 1:7; 9:10).

### v.20
- Paronomasia **שׁוֹמֵר / יַשְׁמִיד** (*shomer / yashmid*), "preserves / destroys" — same God guards covenant-keepers and judges covenant-breakers; mercy and justice in one character.
- "Love him" parallels "fear him" (v.19) — fear and love are not opposed in covenant thinking (Deut 10:12). Two-ways binary (Ps 1; Prov 1-9).

### v.21
- **תְּהִלַּת יְהוָה יְדַבֶּר־פִּי** — chiastic word order; inclusio with v.1's *aromimkha*.
- "All flesh" (*kol-basar*) — embodied creatures (Gen 2 dust) drawn into the final chorus; bridges to Pss 146-150 ("everything that has breath," 150:6).

## Discussion

### Observation
1. List every verb whose subject is YHWH in vv.14-20. What kind of action do the participles describe?
   - **Leader's note:** *somek, zoqef, potech, shomer* are active participles — continuous, habitual action. Providence is sustained personal involvement, not deistic clockwork.
2. Count the occurrences of "all" (*kol*) in these eight verses. What is the cumulative effect?
   - **Leader's note:** ~11×. The repetition hammers universal scope — falling, living things, callers, lovers, wicked, flesh. The whole cosmos is in view.
3. Trace the body parts named across the section. What sequence do they form?
   - **Leader's note:** eyes (v.15) → hand (v.16) → mouth (v.21): creature looks, God gives, creature praises — the providence-and-response cycle.
4. How does v.21 connect back to v.1 of the psalm?
   - **Leader's note:** Inclusio — "I will extol" (v.1) → "my mouth will speak the praise" (v.21), then widened to "all flesh." Personal vow becomes cosmic chorus.

### Interpretation
5. Why pair "righteous in all his ways" with "kind in all his works" (v.17)?
   - **Leader's note:** Trustworthy providence flows from trustworthy character; resists both "just but harsh" and "loving but capricious." Mirrors the vv.8-9 credo.
6. What does "in truth" (v.18) add to "all who call on him"?
   - **Leader's note:** *Be'emet* — sincerity is the qualifier, not religious pedigree; guards against rote invocation (Isa 29:13). Nearness is granted to honest petitioners.
7. Why does the psalm keep v.20b — "all the wicked he will destroy"?
   - **Leader's note:** Covenant-justice, not vindictiveness. For the powerless under oppressors, a King who distinguishes loyal from violator is lifeline. Don't edit it out.
8. What did "you open your hand" (v.16) mean to an agrarian people, versus a regional fertility deity?
   - **Leader's note:** YHWH, not Baal/Hadad, governs the harvest calendar; provision is personal and "in due season." Universalized to every living thing.

### Application
9. When your fridge is full, do you credit your career or YHWH's open hand (v.16)? When empty, whom do you blame?
   - **Leader's note:** Application §2 "functional self-sufficiency" — re-receive food/breath/rest as gift, not acquisition.
10. Is your prayer "in truth" (v.18), or has it gone formulaic? Would you pray differently if no one were listening?
    - **Leader's note:** Application §2 "performance-based prayer"; habit — the weekly "in-truth audit."
11. What bowed-down burden (v.14) — yours or another's — have you stopped expecting God to lift?
    - **Leader's note:** Application §2 "despair over the bowed-down"; habit — adopt one bowed-down person this month as an instrument of YHWH's lifting.
12. How might you become a "junior partner" in the open hand (v.16) toward the hungry and falling?
    - **Leader's note:** Application — imitate the open hand through generosity; providence makes the disciple a conduit, not just a recipient.
