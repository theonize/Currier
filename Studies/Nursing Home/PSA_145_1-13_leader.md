# Leader's Notes — Psalms 145:1-13 - I will extol you, my King

## Verses

> **1** I will extol you, my God and King, and bless your name forever and ever.
>
> **2** Every day I will bless you and praise your name forever and ever.
>
> **3** Great is the LORD, and greatly to be praised, and his greatness is unsearchable.
>
> **4** One generation shall commend your works to another, and shall declare your mighty acts.
>
> **5** On the glorious splendor of your majesty, and on your wondrous works, I will meditate.
>
> **6** They shall speak of the might of your awesome deeds, and I will declare your greatness.
>
> **7** They shall pour forth the fame of your abundant goodness and shall sing aloud of your righteousness.
>
> **8** The LORD is gracious and merciful, slow to anger and abounding in steadfast love.
>
> **9** The LORD is good to all, and his mercy is over all that he has made.
>
> **10** All your works shall give thanks to you, O LORD, and all your saints shall bless you!
>
> **11** They shall speak of the glory of your kingdom and tell of your power,
>
> **12** to make known to the children of man your mighty deeds, and the glorious splendor of your kingdom.
>
> **13** Your kingdom is an everlasting kingdom, and your dominion endures throughout all generations. [The LORD is faithful in all his words and kind in all his works.]
>
> -- *English Standard Version (ESV)*

---

## Overview

- **Setting** — Traditionally David (c. 1010-970 BC); polished acrostic may reflect post-exilic final shaping. Implicit venue: Jerusalem temple-courts, but scope is cosmic.
- **Author / speaker** — David, Israel's king, who steps off his own throne to bow to the true King.  The *only* psalm titled **תְּהִלָּה** (*tehillah*, "Praise") — the singular of *Tehillim*, the Hebrew name for the whole Psalter.
- **Position in the book** — David's last superscribed psalm; the doorway into the closing Hallel pentad (Pss 146-150).  Caps Book V and the entire Psalter.
- **Genre / frame** — Hymn of descriptive praise (extolling who God is, not one rescue act).  Alphabetic acrostic (א-ת) = praise "from A to Z," signaling completeness.  Also catechetical and royal-praise.
- **Big idea** — YHWH alone is the everlasting King, and his greatness is anchored in covenant mercy; therefore praise him comprehensively, daily, and across generations.
- **Supporting movements:**
  - vv.1-2 — personal vow to bless God's name forever.
  - vv.3-7 — God's unsearchable greatness, declared generation to generation.
  - vv.8-9 — chiastic center: the Exodus 34:6 credo of grace.
  - vv.10-13 — saints proclaim the everlasting kingdom to all humanity.
- **Structure** — concentric expansion: self (vv.1-2) → generation (v.4) → saints (v.10) → cosmos (v.9) → eternity (v.13), spiraling around the credal center.

## Info

### v.1
- **אֲרוֹמִמְךָ** (*aromimkha*) — Polel imperfect of *rum*, "I will exalt/lift high"; intensive, iterative.
- "my God and King" — *Elohai hammelek*; articulated "*the* King" distinguishes YHWH from all rivals.
- Royal-coronation diction redirected from human kings to YHWH.
- **לְעוֹלָם וָעֶד** (*le'olam wa'ed*), "forever and ever" — doubled merism of duration; frames the psalm (inclusio with v.13).

### v.2
- "Every day" — the *tamid* rhythm of morning/evening sacrifice (Exodus 29:38-42); unbroken daily devotion.
- Repetition of *barak* ("bless," vv.1, 2, 10) — praise as continuous posture, not occasion.

### v.3
- "Great... greatly... greatness" — polyptoton on *gadol/gedullah*, piling the root for effect.
- **אֵין חֵקֶר** ("no searching") — God's greatness is categorically *unsearchable*; worship is the rational posture before infinity (cf. Job 5:9; Rom 11:33).

### v.4
- **דּוֹר לְדוֹר** (*dor ledor*), "generation to generation" — formulaic Hebrew for unbroken transmission; practical catechesis (Deut 6:6-9; Ps 78:3-4).
- **גְּבוּרוֹת** (*geburot*), "mighty acts" — ANE kings boasted of these (Tel Dan, Mesha steles); the psalm transfers the category exclusively to YHWH.

### v.5
- Triple-stack **הָדָר כְּבוֹד הוֹד** (*hadar kebod hod*) — "splendor / glory / majesty," heaped royal epithets.
- "I will meditate" (*siach*) — wisdom-tradition contemplation (Pss 1:2; 119:15); filled reflection, not blank emptying.

### v.6
- "Awesome deeds" (*nor'ot*, root *yr'*) — things that evoke holy fear.
- Interweave of "they shall... and I will" — corporate prediction braided with personal vow.

### v.7
- **זֵכֶר** (*zeker*), "fame/memorial" — in an honor-shame, oral culture, praise = public reputation traveling by speech.
- "Pour forth... sing aloud" — exuberant, overflowing testimony.

### v.8
- **Chiastic center**; cites Exodus 34:6 (Sinai self-revelation), the OT's most-repeated credo (Num 14:18; Neh 9:17; Ps 86:15; 103:8; Joel 2:13; Jonah 4:2).
- **חַנּוּן וְרַחוּם** (*channun werachum*) — word order flipped from Exodus to satisfy the acrostic (*chet*: *channun*).
- **אֶרֶךְ אַפַּיִם** (*erekh apayim*), "slow to anger" = lit. "long of nostrils"; anatomical wit for divine patience.
- **חֶסֶד** (*chesed*) — covenant steadfast love; the defining relational attribute.

### v.9
- "Good to all... mercy over all he has made" — universal scope; Israel's particular King is the cosmic Sovereign (cf. Matt 5:45; Acts 14:17).
- **רַחֲמִים** (*rachamim*) from **רֶחֶם** (*rechem*, "womb") — gut-deep, maternal compassion (Isa 49:15).

### v.10
- "All your works... all your saints" — personified creation joins the *chasidim* (those who reciprocate *chesed*).
- Presumes the gathered covenant community in corporate worship.

### v.11-12
- **מַלְכוּת** (*malkut*, "kingdom") clusters here — central theme.
- **בְּנֵי הָאָדָם** (*benei ha'adam*), "children of man" — universal audience crossing ethnic Israel (foreshadows Gen 12:3).
- Praise overflows into proclamation/testimony.

### v.13
- "Everlasting kingdom... all generations" — political theology for a people whose Davidic throne lay in ruins; YHWH's throne outlasts every empire.
- Bracketed **nun** line restored from 11QPsa, LXX, Peshitta; MT lost it by *parablepsis* (eye-skip). ESV brackets to mark textual transparency.
- Load-bearing canonical beam: Gen 17:7 → Dan 7:14 → Luke 1:33 → Rev 11:15.

## Discussion

### Observation
1. Trace every "I will" and "they shall" in vv.1-13. How does the speaker move from himself outward?
   - **Leader's note:** Surface the concentric expansion — self (vv.1-2) → generations (v.4) → saints (v.10) → all creation (v.9) → eternity (v.13). The psalm begins with one mouth and ends with all generations.
2. What words for God's greatness, glory, and might can you list from these verses?
   - **Leader's note:** Maximalist diction — greatness, splendor, majesty, wondrous works, mighty/awesome deeds, power, glory. The acrostic form itself signals "praise from A to Z."
3. Where is the literal center of vv.1-13, and what does it say about God?
   - **Leader's note:** vv.8-9, the Exodus 34:6 credo — gracious, merciful, slow to anger, abounding in steadfast love, good to all. The chiastic pivot grounds "greatness" in mercy.
4. How often, and how long, does the psalmist commit to praise?
   - **Leader's note:** "Every day" (v.2) and "forever and ever" (vv.1, 2, 13) — daily rhythm plus eternal horizon; the *tamid* pattern.

### Interpretation
5. Why does David — a king himself — call YHWH "my God and King"?
   - **Leader's note:** Collapses the ANE separation of human king and patron deity. Even the throne-holder confesses authority is borrowed. Cf. 1 Sam 8:7.
6. What does "unsearchable" (v.3) mean for how we worship?
   - **Leader's note:** *Ein cheqer* — God's greatness can never be exhausted by the creature; worship is the fitting posture before infinity, not a problem to solve.
7. Why does the psalm put the mercy-credo (vv.8-9) at the structural center?
   - **Leader's note:** So "King" is never read as tyrant. Sovereignty is defined by the disposition of Exodus 34 — power wedded to covenant tenderness.
8. What is at stake in "your kingdom is an everlasting kingdom" (v.13) for a people without a throne?
   - **Leader's note:** Post-exilic lifeline — allegiance to a reign that outlasts Cyrus, Alexander, Caesar. Bridges to Daniel 7 and the NT kingdom proclamation.

### Application
9. The psalm moves from "I" (v.1) to "all generations" (v.13). Who is downstream of your faith?
   - **Leader's note:** Pull from Application §2 "privatized faith" / "generational neglect." Press: have you declared God's mighty acts (v.4) to anyone recently?
10. Do your mental categories for God's "greatness" reach naturally for his "mercy" (v.8)? Or do they feel like two different gods?
    - **Leader's note:** Application §2 "imbalance between greatness and mercy" — neither sentimentalize nor tyrannize. Hold vv.3 and 8 together.
11. v.5 commands meditation, not consumption. What would ten minutes of *siach* on one work of God look like this week?
    - **Leader's note:** Application habit #3 — dwell on one work without scrolling on; distinguish meditation from content-consumption.
12. When you scroll the news, do you leave more anxious or more anchored (v.13)?
    - **Leader's note:** Application §2 "despair before earthly kingdoms" — re-anchor allegiance in the everlasting kingdom; empires are time-bound subsets of it.
