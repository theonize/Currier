# Hear, O Israel
#### handout

## A Group Study on Deuteronomy 6:1-9

> "Now this is the commandment, the statutes and the rules that the LORD your God commanded me to teach you, that you may do them in the land to which you are going over, to possess it,
> that you may fear the LORD your God, you and your son and your son's son, by keeping all his statutes and his commandments, which I command you, all the days of your life, and that your days may be long.
> Hear therefore, O Israel, and be careful to do them, that it may go well with you, and that you may multiply greatly, as the LORD, the God of your fathers, has promised you, in a land flowing with milk and honey.
>
> "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God, the LORD is one.
> You shall love the LORD your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your might.
> And these words that I command you today shall be on your heart.
> You shall teach them diligently to your children, and shall talk of them when you sit in your house, and when you walk by the way, and when you lie down, and when you rise.
> You shall bind them as a sign on your hand, and they shall be as frontlets between your eyes.
> You shall write them on the doorposts of your house and on your gates."
>
> *(Deuteronomy 6:1-9, ESV)*

*A note about Moses:* He was 120 years old, standing on the plains of Moab, preaching the longest sermon in the Bible to a generation born in the wilderness. He would not cross the Jordan with them. Before he died, he gathered the whole nation and pressed into their hearts the words we now call *the Shema* — after the Hebrew word that opens it, *Sh'ma*, "Hear." Observant Jews still recite these verses every morning and every evening, and pray them as the last words on their lips when they die. When a teacher of the law later asked Jesus which commandment was greatest, Jesus quoted these verses. He called them *the* greatest — not one of the greatest. They give us a confession (who God is), a command (how to love Him), and a method (how to keep that love alive across the years and generations).

---

## 1. Hear, O Israel — The One Lord (v.4)

The first word is not *do* — it is *hear*. The Hebrew word *shema* (שְׁמַע) does not mean "listen with the ears." It means *listen so as to obey*. In Hebrew there is no separate word for "obey" — to truly hear is to do what was said. Religion does not begin with our action; it begins with God speaking and us listening.

Then comes the great confession: *the LORD is one*. The Hebrew *echad* means single, unique, undivided. Israel was about to enter a land filled with many gods — a god of rain, a god of crops, a god of war. Into that crowded sky, Moses says: there is only One. He has no rival, and no part of His character contradicts another part.

**For us:** Many of you have spent your whole life learning to *hear*. The world is loud — the radio, the worries, the news of children and grandchildren. Underneath all of it, one Voice has been speaking. In old age, much gets stripped away. Hearing fails. Mobility fails. Friends die. Memories blur. But *one* thing does not change: *the LORD is one*. You have known Him in joy, you have known Him in grief — and all of those seasons were the same Lord. When the day comes that you cannot remember much else, these few words will still be true: *Hear. The LORD our God, the LORD is one.* Many saints have died with these words on their lips. They are a worthy first prayer in the morning and a worthy last prayer at night.

### Questions

1. Across the long years of your life, where have you most clearly seen that the LORD is *one* — that He has been the same God in every season?
2. What does it mean for you, *today*, to *hear* before you *do*? What is the Lord asking you to listen to right now?

---

## 2. Love the LORD with All (vv.5-6)

After the confession of who God is comes the command of what we owe Him — and the command is *love*. Moses names three spheres — heart, soul, might — and puts the word *all* in front of each one. **Heart** (*levav*) is the inner person — will, mind, intention, emotion together. **Soul** (*nephesh*) is the whole living self, the breath, the appetites, the life. **Might** (*me'od*) literally means "very-much-ness" — everything you've got. The ancient rabbis said *me'od* meant even "with all your money," because possessions reveal whose we are. When Jesus quoted the verse and added "*mind*," He was unfolding what was already there.

And then v.6: "*these words shall be on your heart*" — not "in" the heart but *on* it, like words written on a tablet that lies open before you. The Word must be on us before it can pass through us.

**For us:** Many of you spent decades doing — working, parenting, building, serving. Love showed up in calluses and casseroles, in tuition checks and prayer lists, in long marriages and longer faithfulness. Now the season has changed. The hands shake. The list is shorter. But love does not retire. *Love with all your heart* — when the body cannot do much, the heart can still adore. *Love with all your soul* — every breath that remains is a breath given back to the One who first breathed it into you. *Love with all your might* — your *might* today may look very small. A whispered prayer. A held hand. A faithful Bible kept open on a side table. In God's economy, these are the *all* of a faithful life. There are saints who can no longer find their way to a familiar room but who can still pray the Lord's Prayer. The words *on the heart* outlast the words in the head.

### Questions

1. Where in your life right now is it easiest to love God? Where is it hardest? What do you think He is teaching you in the harder place?
2. What words of Scripture, hymns, or prayers are *on your heart* — words you could still say if everything else were taken away? What does it tell you that those particular words have stayed?

---

## 3. Pass It On (vv.7-9)

After confession, command, and internalization comes *transmission*. What is on your heart must be passed to the next heart. The Hebrew word for "teach diligently" is *shinnen* — literally *sharpen, whet*, like sharpening a knife by repeated strokes. Teach by repeating until the words cut into the listener.

Then Moses lists every place and time of daily life — *sit, walk, lie down, rise; hand, eyes, doorposts, gates*. The point is not religious activity at scheduled times. It is *saturation*: God's Word woven into every posture, every hour, every threshold of life. *Sit, walk, lie down, rise* covers every position of the day. *Hand* and *eyes* cover all you do and all you see. *Doorposts* and *gates* cover home and city, private life and public life. There is no place where God's Word does not belong.

**For us:** Many of you no longer raise children — but children and grandchildren still belong to your prayers and your words. A grandmother who prays the Shema over a granddaughter she rarely sees is doing the very work commanded in Deuteronomy 6. A grandfather who tells a grandson, *"Let me tell you what God has done for me,"* is teaching the way Moses commanded. A short note, a phone call, a remembered birthday card with a verse on it — these are doorposts. Small wood; large effect. Some of you cannot reach grandchildren by distance or by fracture. Pray for them by name. Speak God's words over them in your room. The Lord who hears in heaven counts those words. The legacy of a faithful elder is not money, not property, not even memory. It is *the words on the heart that get passed to the next heart.* That is what outlasts a casket.

### Questions

1. Who taught *you* about the Lord in your earliest years? What words or stories from that teaching are still alive in you today?
2. Who are the children, grandchildren, neighbors, or younger believers in *your* life right now? In what small ways could you "talk of these things" with them in this season?

---

### For Reflection

Take three things home from Deuteronomy 6 this week:

**First — Hear before you do.** Each morning, before the day's worries take over, take a quiet moment to *hear*. Read a verse. Pray the Shema: *"Hear, O Israel: the LORD our God, the LORD is one."* Let the *one* unchanging God set the tone for the whole day.

**Second — Love with all of what you have today.** Do not measure your love for God by what you used to do. Measure it by what you give Him *now* — the small prayers, the patient suffering, the kind word, the open Bible. The widow's two coins were *all* she had. That was the *all* He praised.

**Third — Speak the words.** Find one person — a child, a grandchild, a friend, a nurse, the believer in the next chair — and say something true about God out loud this week. *"The Lord has been faithful to me." "God is good." "He has never let me go."* Doorposts do not need to be elaborate. They just need to be *up*.

The God who spoke these words to Moses on the plains of Moab is the same God who speaks them to you today. *Hear, O Israel: the LORD our God, the LORD is one.*

That is the first word of the morning. That is the last word of the night. That is the song of every faithful life — from cradle to grave, and into glory.
