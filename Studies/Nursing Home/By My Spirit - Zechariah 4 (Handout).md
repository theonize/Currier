# By My Spirit

## A Group Study on Zechariah 4:1-14

> "I see, and behold, a lampstand all of gold, with a bowl on the top of it, and seven lamps on it... And there are two olive trees by it..."
>
> "Not by might, nor by power, but by my Spirit, says the LORD of hosts. Who are you, O great mountain? Before Zerubbabel you shall become a plain. And he shall bring forward the top stone amid shouts of 'Grace, grace to it!'"
>
> *(Selected from Zechariah 4:1-7, ESV)*

*A note about Zechariah:* He was a young prophet who served the discouraged remnant who came back from exile to rebuild the temple. The work had stalled for 16 years. The people felt small, tired, and surrounded by mountains they could not move. Into that weariness, God gave Zechariah a vision of a golden lampstand fed by two olive trees — and one of the most quoted promises in the Old Testament: *"Not by might, nor by power, but by my Spirit."*

---

## 1. The Lamp That Doesn't Run Out (vv.2-3, 14)

Zechariah saw a golden lampstand with two olive trees beside it. Oil flowed from the trees through golden pipes into the lamp — automatically, continually, without anyone having to refill it. The angel called the two olive trees "the anointed ones" (literally, "sons of the oil"). They were not generators of light. They were channels of supply.

**For us:** You don't have to be strong to be a lamp. You only have to be connected to the oil. Many of us spent our lives feeling like we had to *generate* something — energy, faithfulness, witness. But the lamp doesn't generate. It receives. Your job today is not to manufacture light. Your job is to keep receiving the Spirit's supply and let Him do the burning.

### Questions
1. Do you tend to think of your spiritual life as a *battery* (with a limited charge) or as a *lamp* (continuously fed by oil)? How does that picture change how you live today?
2. Who are the "olive trees" through whom God has poured His Spirit into your life? Pause and thank God for them.

---

## 2. Not by Might, Nor by Power (v.6)

Zerubbabel had a temple to rebuild, no army, little money, tired workers, and powerful enemies. God's word to him was not "try harder" but **"Not by might, nor by power, but by my Spirit, says the LORD of hosts."** Three Hebrew words for power, climbing higher: *might* (armies), *power* (personal strength), *Spirit* (the very breath of God). The temple would be finished — but not by Zerubbabel's resources.

**For us:** Many of us spent decades depending on strong arms, strong minds, strong wills. Now those things slow down. The world calls this loss. But the verse calls it the moment we finally see what was always true: God's work is done by God's Spirit. The most powerful prayers in this building are sometimes prayed by those who can do the least. "Not by might" is good news for tired hearts.

### Questions
1. When you face a worry, is your first instinct to solve it with your own resources, or to ask the Spirit to act?
2. In what season of your life have you most clearly seen God do something that was *not* by your might or power, but by His Spirit?

---

## 3. The Mountain Becomes a Plain (v.7)

A "great mountain" stood in front of Zerubbabel — opposition, debris, discouragement, delay. God did not promise to help him climb it. God promised the mountain itself would become a *plain*. And when the final capstone went up on the finished temple, the people would not shout "Look what we did!" — they would shout, "**Grace, grace to it!**"

**For us:** What is your "great mountain" tonight? A diagnosis. A grandchild far from God. A grief that won't lift. A fear of what comes next. Zechariah 4 invites you to look that mountain in the eye and say, in the name of the Lord of hosts, "Who are you, O great mountain?" The mountain has an appointment with Grace. And whether the answer comes in this life or the next, the final cry of every Christian life is the same: *Grace, grace!*

### Questions
1. What is the "great mountain" in your life right now? What would it look like to *speak to it* in faith rather than just stare at it?
2. Looking back over your life, where have you seen God turn a mountain into a plain?

---

### For Reflection

This week, take three things home from Zechariah 4:

**First — Be a lamp, not a battery.** Each morning, take a quiet moment simply to *receive* — the Word, the Spirit, the love of God. The lamp does not generate light. It receives oil. So do you.

**Second — Pray Zechariah 4:6.** Before any worry, any prayer, any task: *"Not by might, nor by power, but by your Spirit, Lord."*

**Third — Speak to the mountain.** Name the mountain that has been weighing on you. And then say it out loud: *"Who are you, O great mountain? Before the Lord of hosts you shall become a plain."*

The temple was finished — not by strength, but by the Spirit. And the final cry was not "Well done, us!" — it was *"Grace, grace to it!"*

That is the cry your whole life is being prepared to shout.
