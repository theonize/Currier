# Not a Spirit of Fear — A Group Study on 2 Timothy 1:1-14

> Paul, an apostle of Christ Jesus by the will of God according to the promise of the life that is in Christ Jesus,
> To Timothy, my beloved child: Grace, mercy, and peace from God the Father and Christ Jesus our Lord.
> I thank God whom I serve, as did my ancestors, with a clear conscience, as I remember you constantly in my prayers night and day.
> As I remember your tears, I long to see you, that I may be filled with joy.
> I am reminded of your sincere faith, a faith that dwelt first in your grandmother Lois and your mother Eunice and now, I am sure, dwells in you as well.
> For this reason I remind you to fan into flame the gift of God, which is in you through the laying on of my hands,
> for God gave us a spirit not of fear but of power and love and self-control.
> Therefore do not be ashamed of the testimony about our Lord, nor of me his prisoner, but share in suffering for the gospel by the power of God,
> who saved us and called us to a holy calling, not because of our works but because of his own purpose and grace, which he gave us in Christ Jesus before the ages began,
> and which now has been manifested through the appearing of our Savior Christ Jesus, who abolished death and brought life and immortality to light through the gospel,
> for which I was appointed a preacher and apostle and teacher,
> which is why I suffer as I do. But I am not ashamed, for I know whom I have believed, and I am convinced that he is able to guard until that Day what has been entrusted to me.
> Follow the pattern of the sound words that you have heard from me, in the faith and love that are in Christ Jesus.
> By the Holy Spirit who dwells within us, guard the good deposit entrusted to you.
> *(2 Timothy 1:1-14, ESV)*

*A note about Paul:* When he wrote this letter, Paul was old, cold, and chained in a Roman dungeon.
He knew his execution was coming within months.
Most of his friends had left him.
From that small stone cell — confined and limited, his strength almost gone — he wrote one last letter to a younger pastor named Timothy.
It is a letter about fear, courage, legacy, and the death-defeating love of Jesus.
It is a letter for people who have been around a long time.

---

## 1. Faith That Was Passed Down (vv.3-5)

Paul begins by remembering.
He thanks God for Timothy's faith — and traces it back two generations to Timothy's mother **Eunice** and his grandmother **Lois**.
Two women, one a widow, raised a pastor.
Lois appears nowhere else in the Bible.
But Paul names her.
She mattered.

- Faith is **handed on**, often by people whose names history forgets
- A grandmother's prayers, a mother's stories, a quiet example — these are real ministry
- Paul prayed for Timothy "night and day" from 800 miles away.  **Prayers travel.**
- You may be the Lois in someone's life, even if you don't see the fruit yet
- Someone, somewhere, almost certainly prayed you into this room

---

## 2. Fan the Flame (vv.6-7)

Paul tells Timothy to **"fan into flame"** the gift God has given him.  The picture is glowing embers — lean in, breathe gently, and the flame jumps back up.  He does not say *light a new fire*. The fire is already there.

Then Paul names the alternative: **a spirit of fear** — cowardice.  And he names what God has actually given us instead:

- **Power** — God's strength, not yours
- **Love** — settled care for others
- **Self-control** — a sound, peaceful mind, not a panicked one

God did not take the gift back when your body slowed down.
The shape changes; the gift remains.

Small breaths on the embers:
- Pray for one person, out loud, by name
- Tell one story of God's faithfulness
- Notice when someone is hurting, and ask

---

## 3. Not Ashamed (vv.8-12)

Paul writes from prison, expecting execution, abandoned by friends. By every worldly measure, his life looks like a failure. And yet he writes:

*"I am not ashamed, for I know whom I have believed."*

Not what — **whom**. Paul's confidence is not in a position. It is in a Person.

And here is the heart of it all (v.10):

> **Christ Jesus...abolished death and brought life and immortality to light.**

"Abolished" does not mean death does not happen. It means death has been **defanged**. The lion is on a leash. The sting is gone. The greatest enemy is already defeated.

- The world honors usefulness — God honors faithfulness
- The world honors strength — God honors trust
- The world fears death — Christ has already abolished it
- If you have believed in him, this is not in question.  The work is done

---

## 4. Guard the Deposit (vv.13-14)

Paul's last word is about stewardship.
The gospel is a **"deposit"** — a treasure placed in trust.
He received it from Christ.
He hands it on to Timothy.
Timothy will hand it on.
And the chain reaches all the way to you, and through you to the next generation.

- The gospel has **content** — it is news with shape, not a feeling
- It is to be **received**, **guarded**, and **handed on** — not invented or rewritten
- You do not guard it alone — the Holy Spirit who lives in you is the chief guardian
- It is called a **good** deposit. Guarding it is a privilege, not a burden

What can you hand on — even from this chair?
- The truth that Jesus saves
- The story of how he saved **you**
- The promise that he keeps what you entrust to him
- The hope of resurrection
- A life that practiced prayer, kindness, and faithfulness

---

## Review & Reflection

1. Who is the Lois or Eunice in your story? Who first taught you about Jesus? Say their name out loud.

2. Whose Lois are you? Who is watching your life and remembering what you say about the Lord — even when you don't realize it?

3. Paul says God gave us a Spirit of **power, love, and self-control** — not of fear. Which of those three do you most need today?

4. What spiritual practice in your life has gone cold? What is one small breath you could give to the embers this week?

5. Verse 10 says Christ has *abolished death*. If you really believed that this morning — plainly, fully — what would change about today?

6. Paul writes "I am not ashamed" from a prison cell. What in your life are you tempted to be ashamed of, that the gospel actually says you should not be?

7. If you wrote a one-page note to your grandchildren — the most important things you have learned about Jesus — what would be on it?

---

### For This Week

- **Fan one ember.** Pick one practice — a prayer, a Psalm, a hymn, a kind word — and do it once a day this week. Small breaths. The flame returns.
- **Make one deposit.** Choose one person and tell them one true thing about Jesus this week. A note, a phone call, a sentence at dinner. You are a guardian of a good deposit. Hand a little of it on.

The Lord who abolished death is able to guard what you entrust to him.
