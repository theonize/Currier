# Clothed in Grace

## A Group Study on Colossians 3:12-17

> Put on then, as God's chosen ones, holy and beloved, compassionate hearts, kindness, humility, meekness, and patience,
> bearing with one another and, if one has a complaint against another, forgiving each other;
> as the Lord has forgiven you, so you also must forgive.
> And above all these put on love, which binds everything together in perfect harmony.
> And let the peace of Christ rule in your hearts, to which indeed you were called in one body.
> And be thankful.
> Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly, teaching and admonishing one another in all wisdom,
> singing psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, with thankfulness in your hearts to God.
> And whatever you do, in word or deed, do everything in the name of the Lord Jesus, giving thanks to God the Father through him.

*A note about Paul:*  When he wrote this letter, he was under house arrest in Rome — a shut-in himself, chained to a guard, confined to a small room.  Yet from that confinement he wrote some of the most joyful words in the Bible. Paul knew what it meant to serve the Lord from a small room.

---

## 1. Who You Are (v.12a)

Before Paul tells the Colossians what to *do*, he tells them who they *are*. Identity comes before action.

- **Chosen** — God picked you. Not because of anything impressive. Because of his love.
- **Holy** — Set apart. God has claimed you as his own.
- **Beloved** — You *have been* loved, and you *remain* loved. It's a settled fact, not a feeling.

### Questions

1. When you hear "chosen, holy, and beloved," which one is hardest for you to believe about yourself? Why?

2. Paul wrote these words while under house arrest. How do you think confinement shaped the way he wrote about identity?

3. If a younger person told you, "I don't feel like God could love me after what I've done," what would you say from this verse?

---

## 2. What You Wear (vv.12b-14)

Paul uses a clothing metaphor: *put on* these qualities the way you put on a garment each morning — a deliberate, daily act.

| Virtue | What It Means | What It Looks Like |
|--------|---------------|--------------------|
| **Compassionate hearts** | Gut-level empathy | Noticing when someone looks sad; asking an aide how her day is going |
| **Kindness** | Goodness that *does* something | A word of encouragement; thanking staff by name |
| **Humility** | Thinking of yourself less | Letting someone else choose the channel |
| **Meekness** | Strength under control | Speaking gently when you're frustrated |
| **Patience** | "Long-tempered" — slow to burn | Waiting without complaining |

Then: **bearing with one another** and **forgiving each other** — "as the Lord has forgiven you." The word for "forgiving" comes from the Greek word for *grace*. To forgive is to "grace" someone.

Over all these: **put on love** — the overcoat that holds everything together.

### Questions

1. "Bearing with one another" means putting up with people who irritate us. Can you think of a time someone put up with *you* patiently?

2. Why does Paul put love *above* all the other virtues — like an overcoat? What happens to a community that has patience and kindness but not love?

---

## 3. How You Live Together (vv.15-16)

**Peace as umpire.** The Greek word for "rule" comes from athletics — an umpire who settles disputes and makes the final call. When competing feelings battle inside you, let Christ's peace make the call.

**The word as resident.** "Dwell" means to *take up permanent residence* — move in and unpack. Not a weekend guest, but a permanent member of the household. How? By teaching one another, lovingly correcting, and singing together.

**And be thankful** — Paul mentions thanksgiving three times in three verses. Gratitude is the atmosphere of the Christian life.

### Questions

1. What is one area of your life right now where you need Christ's peace to step in and make the final call?

2. Thanksgiving appears three times in vv.15-17.  Why does Paul hammer this point? What is the connection between gratitude and peace?

---

## 4. Everything (v.17)

*"Whatever you do, in word or deed, do everything in the name of the Lord Jesus."*

"In the name of" means *under his authority, as his representative, for his honor* — like a diplomat who acts in the name of her country.

There is no such thing as a small act done in the name of Jesus:

- Eating breakfast — *in the name of Jesus*
- Receiving help getting dressed — *in the name of Jesus*
- Praying for a grandson who has wandered — *in the name of Jesus*
- Saying a kind word to a nurse having a hard day — *in the name of Jesus*
- Sitting quietly with a roommate who is dying — *in the name of Jesus*

And the final word is *thanks*. Not accomplishment. Not productivity. Thanks.

### Questions

1. What is one ordinary daily activity you've never thought of as done "in Jesus' name"? How does that change the way you see it?

2. Looking back over the whole passage (vv.12-17), what is one thing God has impressed on your heart? What will you carry with you?
